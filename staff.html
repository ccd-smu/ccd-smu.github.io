Frequently Asked Questions

Placement queries

When should I submit the placement submission template?


You are encouraged to submit the placement submission template within 1 month from the conclusion of the club membership,activities/events/competitions/workshops as students will be looking out for the entry to appear in ICMS.

What should I include for placement submission(s)?


Do include the following:

  • • Club Membership & Leadership
  • • Activities/events/workshops held by the clubs that is also open to SMU participants outside of the club
  • • Activities/events/ workshops/competitions that are conducted externally but verifiable by staff (e.g., students representing SMU in an external competition)

    • Do NOT include the following:

  • • Activities/events/workshops that are a regular part of the memberships (e.g., weekly meetings, training sessions, Annual General Meetings)
  • • Activities/events that are counted towards the fulfilment of Community Service Hours, as it will be recorded by the C4SR department

    • Is targeting more GLOs better for students?


    Targeting more GLOs may not necessarily mean it is better for students.

    The objective of the CCT is to enable students to engage in purposeful co-curricular learning, so it is more meaningful to only indicate the GLOs relevant to the activity.

    How should I decide on what GLOs to select?

    Prior to the start of the activity/ academic year, you should discuss with the student leader(s) which GLOs the CCA group should aim to develop/ focus on.

    When should my students proceed to write their reflection(s)?


    Students may complete their reflections anytime upon the successful upload of the placements.

    Who should I include for the placement submission(s)?


    Only undergraduate students who enrolled from year 2021 onwards.

    You may follow this email format when you submit placements: (studentname)(year)@(school).smu.edu.sg

    E.g., john.doe.2021@business.smu.edu.sg

    Assessment queries

    What are the 3 different levels of assessment?


    Please download and refer to the Transformative Learning Rubric.

    Why should I assess students?


    Assessing your students allows you to recognise and endorse those who have contributed meaningfully to your club or activity.

    When should I assess students?


    You may assess the students when they meet any of the following:

  • • Served as student leaders
  • • Submitted a Post-Activity Reflection
  • • Demonstrated personal growth from participating in the co-curricular experience

    • What happens if I do not assess the students?


    Students with no staff assessments will be given a default assessment of “Exposure”, akin to participation, for the selected GLOs.

    Do my assessment need to be the same for every GLOs?


    Assessment levels can vary for each GLO selected. For example, a student may be assessed as “Transformation” for GLO2 – Intellectual and Creative Skills, but only as “Exposure” for GLO5 – Personal Mastery.
