You probably have heard this before : A bit of stress is good.

A little bit of stress is not bad; Positive stress could give you energy, motivation and spark creativity; Also makes you more alert and helps you perform better in certain situations.

Stress is only beneficial if it is short-lived. Excessive or prolonged stress can lead to illnesses such as heart disease and mental health problems such as anxiety and depression.

Eustress (Positive Stress)

Motivates, focuses energy
Is perceived as within our coping abilities
Feels exciting
Improves performance
Distress (Negative Stress)

Causes anxiety or concern
Is perceived as outside of our coping abilities
Feels unpleasant
Decreases performance
Can lead to mental and physical problems
Negative stressors
  • The death of a loved one
  • Injury or illness (oneself or a family member)
  • Conflict in interpersonal relationships
  • Feeling unprepared for tests or exams
  • Financial problems
  • Class presentations/ public speaking
  • Fears (fear of flying, heights, meeting new people)
  • Lack of social self-confidence
  • Unrealistic expectations
  • Procrastination and failing to plan ahead
  • Over scheduling
  • Conflicts with close friends
Common Signs of Negative Stress
Changes in your body
  • Tense muscles
  • Rapid breathing and heart rate
  • Headaches
  • Difficulties sleeping well
  • Fatigue
Changes in your behaviours
  • Withdrawing from others
  • Fidgeting, feeling restless
  • Smoking, drinking or using more drugs than usual
  • Avoiding situations that you think are stressful
Changes in your feelings
  • Feeling worried or confused
